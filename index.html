What is wine?

Wine is defined for legal purposes as “a water and alcohol
solution obtained from the complete or partial fermentation
of fresh grapes or grape must.” Wine is made with
particular types of grapes known as Vitis Vinifera, otherwise
called wine grapes, that are small, sweet,
and have thicker skins.

Composition

The basic ingredients of wine are water and alcohol,
though other components present in miniscule quantities
have a determining effect on quality and character.
Wine consists of:
Water (80%-85%)
Alcohols (10%-20%)
Acids (0.4%-1%)
Sugars (0.1% (1 g/l)-10% (100 g/l)
Mineral salts (0.2%-0.4%)
Coloring substances (0.01 %-0.5 %)
Aromatic substances (0.01%-0.1%)

Types of wine

Full-bodied red wines

(CABERNET SAUVIGNON, SYRAH,
CABERNET FRANC)


They have more tannins and are richer in alcohol
content. The tannins are felt at the back of your
tongue as a grippy sensation that dries your
mouth and pulls it together.

Medium-bodied red wines

(MERLOT, BARBERA)


Best known for being a great accompaniment to
almost any food. If you’re not too fond of full-
bodied wines, you can pair medium-bodied reds
with heavier foods just as well.

Light-bodied red wines

(PINOT NOIR, GAMAY, BLAUFRÄNKISCH)


The lightest of the reds are made from brighter,
thinner skinned grapes and have the least
tannins. Still, they can be paired with many
foods, such as cheese.

Rosé wines

(GRENACHE, SANGIOVESE, MOURVÈDRE)


The middle ground between red and white wine
is called a Rosé – your perfect summer wine.
Rosé pairs flawlessly with spicier foods, such as
Thai or Mexican.

Full-bodied white wines

(OAKED CHARDONNAY, RIBOLLA GIALLA)


These are usually wines that had undergone
ageing in oak barrels and a second, malolactic,
fermentation. The most typical representative of
full-bodied white wine is Chardonnay.

Light-bodied white wines

(SAUVIGNON BLANC, ZELEN, PINELA,
PINOT GRIS, RIESLING ITALICO)


Crisp dry wines that are a prime choice for those
who enjoy a touch of liveliness on the tongue.
You will enjoy them the most in hot weather
along with a fresh salad or sushi.
